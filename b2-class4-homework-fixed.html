Small Talk at Work • Indirect Questions

This homework only practices Class 4 content: indirect WH-questions, indirect yes/no questions with if/whether, and workplace-introduction vocabulary.

1. Reading

Meeting a New Employee

A receptionist welcomes a new employee to the office. She asks him to take a seat and explains that the Recruitment Manager will arrive soon. The new employee says he feels a little nervous because it is his first day.

The receptionist makes small talk and asks polite questions. Instead of asking direct questions like “Where do you report?” she says, “Could you tell me who you report to?” She also asks, “Do you know if you already have your staff pass?”

The employee says he is still waiting for some paperwork, but he is starting to settle in. The receptionist smiles and says that most people feel more comfortable after the first week.

2. Vocabulary

Match the workplace word or phrase to its meaning.

Real-life English tip: In a workplace, polite questions often sound better than direct questions, especially when you are meeting someone for the first time.

3. Writing

Write a conversation between new employees at work (6–8 lines). Use at least 2 Indirect Questions and some class vocabulary.
